VekilAI Analysis

Risk Analysis and Strategic Interpretation for Client x Inc.

Article 7.1: Intellectual Property Rights

Finding: The article stipulates the transfer of all intellectual property developed by the client to the other party. This means the client would lose the right to use their own developed codebase, libraries, and methodologies in future projects.

Strategic Interpretation: This clause poses the highest risk to the client's business continuity. During negotiation, it should be demanded that the ownership of the client's "Pre-Existing Intellectual Property" remains with them, and the customer is granted only a "license" to use the project deliverables.

Article 11.1: Unilateral Termination Right

Finding: An indefinite right of termination without cause is granted to the other party. This creates the risk of the contract ending abruptly before the client can recoup their investment and allocated resources.

Strategic Interpretation: The right to terminate should be tied to the existence of "just cause," or it should be stipulated that if the other party exercises its right to terminate without cause, the client will be reimbursed for all expenses incurred to date plus a specific "termination fee."

Article 5.1: Ambiguous Payment Term

Finding: The phrase "successful completion" is subjective and open to interpretation. The other party could arbitrarily claim the project was not successful to delay or refuse payment.

Strategic Interpretation: Payment should not be tied to an abstract concept like "success" but to concrete, measurable, and documented milestones, such as "completion of the analysis phase," "end of testing," or "go-live."

Article 9.1: Disproportionate Confidentiality Period

Finding: An "unlimited period" of confidentiality is contrary to commercial practice and creates a disproportionate burden for the client. It is impractical for this obligation to persist for information that becomes public knowledge or loses its relevance over time.

Strategic Interpretation: It should be requested that the confidentiality obligation be limited to a reasonable period (e.g., 3 or 5 years) after the contract's termination. A longer period can be negotiated for information that qualifies as a trade secret.

Overall Assessment: This report identifies the asymmetrical risk distribution in the agreement and the ambiguities that are detrimental to the client. The points raised form the basis of strategic arguments to be used during negotiation to strengthen your client's position and prevent potential legal disputes.
